The global under-five mortality rate has fallen by roughly 60 percent since
1990, from about 93 deaths per 1,000 live births to about 37 per 1,000 by 2022
(UN Inter-agency Group for Child Mortality Estimation, "Levels and Trends in
Child Mortality 2023").

Leading drivers of the decline:

1. Expanded immunization, which cut deaths from vaccine-preventable disease
   (WHO, "Immunization coverage" fact sheet, 2023).

2. Better treatment of diarrheal disease and pneumonia through oral rehydration
   therapy and wider access to care (UNICEF data pages, 2023).

3. Improved maternal and newborn care, which lowered neonatal mortality
   (UNICEF and WHO joint estimates, 2023).

The exact share attributable to any single factor is uncertain because the
drivers overlap and vary by region, so these are best read as leading
contributors rather than a precise ranking.
